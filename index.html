Catagories of CTF Compitition

CTF (Capture The Flag) competitions typically encompass various categories of challenges designed to test participants' skills in cybersecurity. Here are some common categories found in CTF competitions.

Web Explanation
Challenges involve exploiting vulnerabilities in web applications, such as SQL injection, Cross-Site Scripting, Cross-Site Request Forgery , and other web-related security flaws.
Cryptography
In this category, participants tackle cryptographic algorithms or solving puzzles. Tasks may include decrypting ciphertexts, cracking hashes, or analyzing cryptographic protocols.
Reverse Engineering
Participants are given executable files or binaries and are tasked with reverse engineering them to understand their functionality, extract hidden flags, or identify vulnerabilities.
Forensics
Participants analyze digital artifacts, such as disk images, packet captures, memory dumps, to extract hidden information or solve challenges related to incidents or cyberattacks.
Binary Exploitation (Pwn)
In this category, participants exploit vulnerabilities in compiled programs, such as buffer overflows, format string vulnerabilities, or other memory corruption issues.
Steganography
Challenges involve hiding messages or data within other files or mediums, such as images, audio files, or network traffic. Participants need to uncover the hidden information.
